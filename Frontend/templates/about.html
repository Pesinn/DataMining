The application has access to over 12.5 million news articles, written in English, published by 89 news sources. Each of these articles have been visible on a landing page of their source. It is possible to apply any search condition to the whole dataset.



Named Entities

100 most frequent named entities can be found for any search condition. It can also be displayed as word cloud

  • DATE - Absolute or relative dates or periods
  • PERSON - People, including fictional
  • GPE - Countries, cities, states
  • LOC - Non-GPE locations, mountain ranges, bodies of water
  • MONEY - Monetary values, including unit
  • TIME - Times smaller than a day
  • PRODUCT - Objects, vehicles, foods, etc. (not services)
  • CARDINAL - Numerals that do not fall under another type
  • ORDINAL - "first", "second", etc.
  • QUANTITY - Measurements, as of weight or distance
  • EVENT - Named hurricanes, battles, wars, sports events, etc.
  • FAC - Buildings, airports, highways, bridges, etc.
  • LANGUAGE - Any named language
  • LAW - Named documents made into laws.
  • NORP - Nationalities or religious or political groups
  • PERCENT - Percentage, including "%"
  • WORK_OF_ART - Titles of books, songs, etc.
Source: https://newscatcherapi.com/blog/named-entity-recognition-with-spacy


Keywords (part-of-speech tagging)

100 most frequent keywords can be found for any search condition.



Sentiment analysis

Sentiment analysis can be displayed for any news articles topic. Also, sentiment analysis can be compared between many different topics. To compare sentiment analysis of e.g. covid and pfizer, both search keywords must be divided by | character. Example: covid | pfizer
